# Commit Readiness Checklist

**Artifact:** Solution Overview Document (SOD)  
**Lifecycle Stage:** Formalize â†’ Commit  
**Purpose:** Verify that intent is sufficiently bounded, constrained, and validated to justify execution.

---

## 1. Intent & Outcome

- [ ] The problem statement is clear, specific, and non-aspirational.
- [ ] The desired outcome is explicitly stated and measurable.
- [ ] Success criteria are unambiguous.
- [ ] The intended audience or user is identified.
- [ ] It is clear why this work should be done now.

---

## 2. Scope & Boundaries

- [ ] In-scope functionality is explicitly defined.
- [ ] Non-goals are clearly stated.
- [ ] Assumptions are documented and reasonable.
- [ ] Dependencies (internal and external) are identified.
- [ ] Scope creep vectors have been considered and constrained.

---

## 3. Constraints & Guardrails

- [ ] Technical constraints are documented.
- [ ] Security constraints are explicit.
- [ ] Compliance or regulatory constraints are identified (if applicable).
- [ ] Operational constraints (support, maintenance, on-call) are acknowledged.
- [ ] Tooling and platform limits are declared.
- [ ] Time and effort boundaries are defined.

---

## 4. Privacy & Policy

- [ ] Privacy level is explicitly declared.
- [ ] Storage locations comply with the privacy level.
- [ ] AI tooling is permitted under the declared privacy level.
- [ ] Collaboration model aligns with privacy constraints.
- [ ] Reclassification implications have been considered.

---

## 5. Architecture & Approach

- [ ] Functional overview is coherent and complete.
- [ ] High-level architecture or workflow is understandable.
- [ ] Major components and interactions are identified.
- [ ] Key trade-offs are acknowledged.
- [ ] No low-level design has leaked in prematurely.

---

## 6. Risks & Unknowns

- [ ] Major risks are identified and documented.
- [ ] Mitigation strategies are plausible.
- [ ] Unknowns are explicitly listed.
- [ ] Required spikes or experiments are identified and bounded.

---

## 7. Execution Framing

- [ ] The first executable increment is defined.
- [ ] The work can begin without inventing requirements.
- [ ] The team (or individual) is capable of executing with current context.
- [ ] Inputs to execution are complete and accessible.

---

## 8. Lifecycle Integrity

- [ ] This work has passed through Capture, Sense, Explore, and Shape.
- [ ] Formalize artifacts are complete and versioned.
- [ ] No unresolved ambiguity remains that would force speculative execution.
- [ ] Regression paths (if needed) are understood.

---

## 9. Commit Decision

- [ ] Proceeding to execution is a deliberate decision.
- [ ] Stakeholders (if any) agree to proceed.
- [ ] The cost of execution is justified by expected value.
- [ ] Abandonment criteria are implicitly understood.

---

## Commit Declaration

By checking all applicable items above, this work is deemed **ready to Commit** and may proceed to governed execution.
